In this workshop, we aim to focus on human-robot interaction controlled mainly via LLMs, with an explicit focus on physical interaction between human and robot in cooperative tasks. In particular, we target three exciting and challenging goals:

  • Building together HRI benchmark and standardization of the metrics and techniques in these studies
  • Contributing to the categorization of the different emerging approaches and techniques
  • Fostering the creation of a community of researchers around the topics of the workshop and promoting research collaboration

The workshop organizers aim to prepare a potential Special Issue, using the insights and discussions generated during the sessions. Participants will be encouraged to actively contribute to the special issue to ensure diverse perspectives and comprehensive coverage of the topics discussed, thereby increasing the impact of the results.

For this reason, the workshop program will alternate interactive activities with panel sessions and keynote presentations. The invited speakers have strong expertise in Human-Robot Interaction with various robotic platforms, with backgrounds ranging from machine learning to cognitive science. Their goal is to share and integrate knowledge to plan future directions and encourage collaboration through oral presentations, round-table discussions, poster sessions, and demonstrations.
